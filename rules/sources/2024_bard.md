## Multiclass traits relevant to Nathan

On entering Bard after Rogue, gain:

- Hit Point Die: D8 per Bard level
- one Skill proficiency of choice
- proficiency with one Musical Instrument of choice
- Light Armor training (already possessed)
- all Bard Level 1 features

Nathan does not gain the three Skills, three Musical Instruments, Saving Throw proficiencies, or starting equipment of a Level 1 starting Bard.

## Nathan at Bard 3

- Proficiency Bonus: +2 from total Character Level 4
- Bardic Inspiration die: d6
- Cantrips: 2
- Prepared Spells: 6
- Level 1 Spell Slots: 4
- Level 2 Spell Slots: 2

## Bardic Inspiration

As a Bonus Action, grant another creature within 60 feet that can see or hear Nathan one d6. It can roll the die after failing a D20 Test within the next hour, potentially changing the failure. One die per creature at a time. Uses equal Charisma modifier, minimum one; all uses return on a Long Rest at Bard 3.

With proposed Charisma 16, Nathan has 3 uses.

## Spellcasting

- two Bard Cantrips
- six prepared Bard Spells of Levels 1–2 in any combination
- Charisma as Spellcasting Ability
- Musical Instrument can serve as Spellcasting Focus
- one Cantrip may be replaced on gaining a Bard level
- one prepared Spell may be replaced on gaining a Bard level

## Level 2 features

### Expertise

Expertise in two existing Skill proficiencies.

### Jack of All Trades

Add half Proficiency Bonus, rounded down, to an ability check using a Skill proficiency Nathan lacks and which does not otherwise use his Proficiency Bonus. At Level 4 this bonus is +1.

## Expertise implementation

- Rogue Expertise: `Deception`, `Perception`
- Bard Expertise: `Performance`, `Animal Handling`

The mechanical result is identical regardless of source at the starting level; this allocation follows the narrative development from thief into bard.

- Bard Multiclass Skill: proposed `Persuasion`
- Bard Multiclass Musical Instrument: proposed Lute
