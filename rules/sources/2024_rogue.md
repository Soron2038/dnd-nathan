## Core traits relevant to Nathan

- Primary Ability: Dexterity
- Hit Point Die: D8 per Rogue level
- Saving Throw proficiencies when starting as Rogue: Dexterity and Intelligence
- Skills when starting as Rogue: choose 4 from Acrobatics, Athletics, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Perception, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand, Stealth
- Weapons: Simple weapons and Martial weapons with Finesse or Light
- Tools: Thieves’ Tools
- Armor: Light armor
- Starting Equipment A: Leather Armor, 2 Daggers, Shortsword, Shortbow, 20 Arrows, Quiver, Thieves’ Tools, Burglar’s Pack, 8 GP
- Starting Equipment B: 100 GP

## Level 1 features

### Expertise

Expertise in two chosen existing Skill proficiencies. The skills need not originate from the Rogue class.

### Sneak Attack

Once per turn, +1d6 damage to one creature hit with an attack roll using a Finesse or Ranged weapon when the attack has Advantage. Advantage is unnecessary when a non-Incapacitated ally is within 5 feet of the target and Nathan does not have Disadvantage. Damage type matches the weapon.

### Thieves’ Cant

Gain Thieves’ Cant and one additional language of choice.

### Weapon Mastery

Choose two proficient weapon kinds and use their mastery properties. Choices may change after a Long Rest.
